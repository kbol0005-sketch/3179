Australia’s Domestic Air Travel Recovery After COVID-19

Australia’s domestic air network connects major hubs such as Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane with regional destinations. The following visualisation shows network density and airport-level traffic changes between 2019 and 2024, highlighting post-pandemic recovery trends.

Passenger Traffic Trends

Domestic passenger volumes have risen steadily since 2021, trending back towards pre-pandemic levels across major routes.

Network Utilisation

ASKs, RPKs and load factors show how much capacity airlines supply and how effectively it’s used. The 2020 dip is followed by a strong rebound from 2021 onward.

This dashboard was created using Vega-Lite and Pure.css as part of an academic visualisation project. Data source: BITRE (2024).
